3 Compliance IT Gaps Costing Indian Mid-Size Banks Crores

By Two Elephants Technologies · 5 min read · May 2025

The biggest risk for mid-size banks is not the presence of regulations — it is the lack of a practical technology roadmap that connects compliance, operations, and audit readiness.

1. Fragmented risk tools without an operating model

Many banks have bought specialist compliance software, but those tools are still isolated from lending, treasury, and payments systems. When a risk incident occurs, teams struggle to trace impact, update controls, and deliver evidence across stakeholders.

2. Manual controls and Excel-based remediation plans

Excel checklists may feel familiar, but they do not support continuous monitoring or escalation. In most cases, remediation remains reactive because teams lack a centralized system that tracks control maturity, responsible owners, and regulatory deadlines.

3. Compliance reporting that isn’t business-ready

Compliance reports are often created for auditors, not for operations leaders. When reports fail to answer practical questions about why a control failed or what the next steps are, the result is repeated audit findings and higher costs.

The most effective programs combine a shared risk taxonomy with a single source of truth for controls, incidents, and remediation workflows. This is what transforms compliance from a cost center into a governance advantage.

How banks can get forward-looking

Start by mapping critical risk domains to existing IT processes, then deploy a lightweight, modular platform that tracks controls across business units. With this approach, mid-size banks can maintain regulatory confidence while scaling digital products.
